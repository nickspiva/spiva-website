# Methodology: Annual Staffing Cost Estimate for Universal Pre-K in APS

**Estimate:** ~$78.2M per year in additional staffing costs  
**Scope:** Operating costs only — teacher and assistant teacher salaries + employer benefits  
**Coverage:** Additional seats needed to achieve universal pre-K for **both 3-year-olds and 4-year-olds** in APS  
**Note:** See caveats — this is not a capital cost estimate and does not include curriculum, materials, transportation, or administrative overhead

---

## Step 1: Estimate the gap in pre-K seats

| Metric | Value | Source / Notes |
|---|---|---|
| APS kindergarten enrollment (2025–26) | 3,620 | [APS Insights enrollment data](https://apsinsights.org/2026/02/23/aps-enrollment-1994-2026/) — used as proxy for size of each age cohort |
| Total seats needed (3K + 4K combined) | 7,240 | 3,620 × 2 cohorts |
| Existing APS pre-K seats | 1,234 | APS Insights, citing GADOE. GADOE does not break out enrollment by age (3 vs. 4 year olds). Note: this figure may not fully reflect Head Start classrooms operated in APS facilities, some of which serve 3-year-olds. |
| **Total gap: additional seats needed** | **6,006** | 7,240 − 1,234 |

*Kindergarten enrollment is used as a proxy for cohort size. This is slightly conservative — some families use private kindergarten or homeschool and may not appear in APS enrollment data. The 3-year-old cohort is assumed to be roughly equal in size to the kindergarten cohort; this is unverified and could be refined using Census estimates of children by single year of age within APS attendance zones.*

## Caveats and Limitations

**Health insurance figure reflects individual/employee-only coverage.**  
The $1,885/month employer premium figure is used for all staff. In practice, employees with family coverage cost the employer considerably more. The $1,885 figure likely represents a single-employee plan; the true average employer cost across the workforce may be higher once family plans are factored in. This estimate may still understate actual health benefit costs.

**Staff costs will rise over the projection period.**  
- The employer pension contribution rate is scheduled to increase to **22.32%** beginning in 2028 (source: TRS of Georgia). Further increases are likely beyond 2028.
- Health insurance premiums historically rise faster than general inflation.
- These rising costs mean the $78.5M figure should be treated as a current-year estimate that will grow in nominal terms each year.

**This is not a capital cost estimate.**  
Providing universal 3K and 4K would require significant capital investment not included here:
- Retrofitting existing classrooms to meet pre-K and 3K licensing standards
- Constructing or leasing 334 additional classrooms
- Furniture, materials, and curriculum for each classroom (~$500–$1,500 per student per year)
- Outdoor play space and playground equipment per licensing requirements

**Additional operating costs not included:**
- Administrative overhead (program coordinator, early learning director, compliance)
- Special education and ESOL support staff
- Transportation (often required for pre-K / 3K programs)
- Professional development and training

**The $100k salary is a target, not current reality.**  
APS has a stated goal of $100k average teacher salary by 2030. Current average is lower. Projections forward to 2055 should account for continued salary growth.

**Enrollment proxy limitations.**  
Kindergarten enrollment undercounts the total eligible population slightly — families using private K or homeschooling are not captured. The true number of eligible 3 and 4 year olds may be modestly higher.

**3K cohort assumption.**  
The estimate assumes roughly equal cohort sizes for 3-year-olds and kindergarteners. This is reasonable but unverified — actual 3-year-old population in APS attendance zones may differ. A more precise figure could be obtained from Census estimates of children by single year of age within APS zip codes.

## Summary

| Scenario | Annual Staffing Cost |
|---|---|
| **Combined 3K + 4K universal pre-K** | **~$78.2M** |

These figures represent **recurring annual staffing costs only** — the salary and employer benefit costs of the additional teachers and assistants required. Capital costs, curriculum, materials, transportation, and administrative overhead would add substantially to the total program cost.
